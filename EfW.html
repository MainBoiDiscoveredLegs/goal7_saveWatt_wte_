WASTE TO ENERGY / ENERGY FROM WASTE

The Basic idea is to convert waste into energy through many different methods. The inspiration for this comes from the sheer number of landfills in India, as mentioned before. The wasted potential that lies within that mound of trash varies from landfill to landfill, but urban India is estimated to produce around 62 million tonnes of waste, which could potentially produce around 33 million MWh of energy.

For reference, a MWh of electrical energy equates to the amount of energy needed to power two average refrigerators for a whole year.

The process of generating electricity in a mass-burn waste-to-energy plant has seven stages :

STEP 1

Waste is dumped from garbage trucks into large pits.

STEP 2

Waste is collected by a claw and is put in a combustion machine.

STEP 3

Waste is burned and releases heat.

STEP 4

Heat turns into water in a boiler.

STEP 5

High pressure steam turns the turbine spinner and produces energy!

EXTRA STEP

The harmful substances in the combustion gas can be diluted with artificial oxygen, to then be released back into the atmosphere.

Most commercial oxygen is produced using a variation of the cryogenic distillation process originally developed in 1895. This process produces oxygen that is 99+% pure. More recently, the more energy-efficient vacuum swing adsorption process has been used for a limited number of applications that do not require oxygen with more than 90-93% purity.
